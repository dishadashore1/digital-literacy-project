Cyberbullying is a form of cybercrime where individuals are harassed, threatened, or humiliated through digital platforms such as social media, messaging apps, or online forums. It often involves repeated negative behavior intended to harm a person emotionally or socially.

For instance, in a typical scenario, a student may become a target of cyberbullying through offensive comments, spreading false information, or sharing private images without consent. For example, a student posts something online, and a group of peers begin to leave abusive comments, create memes, or circulate rumors through group chats. The victim may feel isolated, embarrassed, and stressed due to the constant harassment.

Young people and students are particularly vulnerable, as they are highly active on social media platforms. The impact of cyberbullying can be severe, leading to anxiety, loss of confidence, academic decline, and in extreme cases, mental health issues.

This issue highlights the importance of responsible digital behavior and awareness. Reporting abusive content, blocking offenders, and promoting respectful communication online are essential steps in preventing cyberbullying. Creating a safe digital environment requires both individual responsibility and collective awareness.
One must strive to treat everyone with respect, and ensure that they don't partake in such a crime.
